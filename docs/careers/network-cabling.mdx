<p className="grey">The range of responsibilities, job roles and skills required of professionals in network cabling</p>

### Responsibilities

`-` Installing, termination, testing and certification of copper and fibre network cable infrastructure <br />
`-` Maintenance of copper and fibre optic cabling <br />
`-` Identify, locate and repair faults in copper and fibre optic network cabling <br />
`-` Installation of equipment cabinets, fixtures/fittings and rack-mounting equipment <br />
`-` Applying physical security measures to network cabling and infrastructure <br />
`-` Carry out a risk assessment (for example health and safety risk assessment) <br />
`-` Working to relevant legislation, standards and industry best practice <br />
`-` Production of clear documentation showing cable route maps, testing and acceptance <br />
`-` Updating asset registers when physical equipment is deployed <br />
`-` Updating maintenance logs when equipment is repaired or updated
`-` Use of service management tools and systems to maintain efficiency and effectiveness through good practice, processes and procedures  <br />


### Job roles
`-` Structured cabling installer/engineer (for example telephony, fibre, data) <br />
`-` Network surveyor <br />
`-` Network analyst <br />
`-` Network installation engineer <br />

### Skills

`-` Manual handling <br />
`-` Working at height <br />
`-` Ability to interpret and follow instructions and plans <br />
`-` Adaptable approach to work <br />
`-` Project management and planning <br />
`-` Prioritisation of tasks and workload <br />
`-` Ability to work alone or as part of a team <br />
`-` Customer service skills <br />
`-` Continuous learning, improving and upskilling <br />
